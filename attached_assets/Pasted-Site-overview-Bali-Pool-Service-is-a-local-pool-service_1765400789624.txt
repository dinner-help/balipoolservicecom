Site overview. Bali Pool Service is a local pool‑service provider for villas, hotels and private homes across Bali.  The homepage positions the company as an experienced team (“10+ years experience” and 500+ happy clients) offering professional pool cleaning and maintenance with emergency response.  The brand promises 24/7 service, WhatsApp customer support and a guarantee that pools remain crystal clear.  However, much of the site is incomplete; many service‑ and location‑pages linked from the footer return a 404 page or incorrectly load another template.  This creates a poor user experience and risks Google perceiving these pages as doorway pages or soft‑404s.  The site lacks author bios, citations, case studies and original images – all factors that have become critical for E‑E‑A‑T and Helpful Content signals in 2025.

Opportunity. With proper technical clean‑up, creation of high‑quality service and location pages and a well‑structured content hub around pool maintenance, construction and Bali‑specific considerations, the domain can grow into the topical authority in the Bali pool‑service niche.  Below is a detailed audit of issues and a growth roadmap.

Thin or absent metadata	Several pages have missing meta titles/descriptions, or generic titles like “Bali Pool Service	Professional Pool Cleaning & Maintenance”.

5 – Technical & on‑page SEO recommendations
	1.	Fix broken pages and routing.  Audit all URLs using Screaming Frog or Sitebulb to identify 404s, 302s and duplicate titles.  Implement 301 redirects from unused slugs (e.g., /pool-cleaning) to the correct page.  Remove internal links pointing to non‑existent pages until those pages are built.
	2.	Consolidate navigation.  Streamline the header and footer menu to link only to core services and categories.  Use a drop‑down for services and another for service contracts; move location links to a dedicated “Service Areas” page rather than listing dozens of links in the footer.
	3.	Structured data.  Implement LocalBusiness schema on every page with NAP information and areaServed for each region.  Use Service schema for each service page (price range, provider).  Use FAQPage on FAQ sections and Review/Rating schema for testimonials.
	4.	Page speed & UX.  Compress hero images and enable lazy-loading.  Remove unused scripts and CSS.  Use proper <h1> and <h2> hierarchies; ensure content is readable on mobile.  Improve core web vitals (LCP < 2.5 s, FID < 100 ms, CLS < 0.1).
	5.	Metadata & alt text.  Write unique meta titles/descriptions for each page incorporating primary keywords and locality; use compelling language and CTAs.  Add descriptive alt attributes to all images (e.g., “technician vacuuming a villa pool in Seminyak”).
	6.	Accessibility & privacy.  Ensure the Accessibility page meets WCAG 2.1 guidelines.  Provide accessible forms and navigation.  Update the Privacy Policy to cover WhatsApp communications and cookie usage.
	7.	Internal linking.  Use contextual internal links within content to connect service pages, blog posts and location pages.  Avoid repetitive exact‑match anchors; instead, use descriptive phrases (“our green‑pool rescue service”).  Add a HTML sitemap linking to major pages.
	8.	Author profiles & E‑E‑A‑T improvements.  Create author pages for each subject‑matter expert (e.g., owner, lead technician) with bios, certifications, and headshots.  Include bylines and updated dates on blog posts and service pages.  Cite authoritative sources (industry guidelines, government health codes) when discussing chemical safety and pool standards.  Publish client case studies with measurable outcomes and quotes.

6 – Off‑page & authority strategy
	1.	Local citations & Google Business Profile.  Optimise and verify Google Business listing with accurate NAP and photos; encourage satisfied clients to leave reviews.  Submit the business to relevant Indonesian and tourism directories (Yellow Pages Indonesia, Bali Expat directories, villa rental platforms).  Build consistent citations across directories like TripAdvisor, Booking.com, HomeAdvisor and Angi.
	2.	Partnerships and collaborations.  Partner with Bali villa rental agencies, property management companies and tourism blogs.  Offer co‑authored guides (e.g., “Pool Care Tips for Villa Owners”) and ask for backlinks.  Sponsor local events or environmental clean‑ups to gain press mentions.
	3.	Industry associations & credentials.  Join and display membership logos from recognised pool maintenance associations (e.g., National Swimming Pool Foundation or local Indonesian associations).  Contribute expert commentary to industry publications.
	4.	Content promotion & link building.  Share educational blog posts on social media (Facebook groups for Bali expats, Instagram, LinkedIn).  Produce short YouTube videos demonstrating pool maintenance tips; embed them in articles.  Pitch guest posts to travel and home‑improvement blogs about pool care in tropical climates.  Encourage satisfied clients to share before/after photos on their social channels with links to your site.
	5.	Review and referral program.  Offer small discounts or maintenance add‑ons for clients who leave honest reviews on Google, TripAdvisor or Facebook.  Implement a referral program where existing customers receive a service credit when referring new clients.
